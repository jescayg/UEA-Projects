Local Attrations

Mauritius has plenty to see and do with numerous local attractions all our the island. For example:

  1. Public beaches – Mauritius island is home to beautiful sandy beaches. The best beaches are Trou aux Biches, Pereybere, Belle Mare and so on. The beaches are blessed with clear blue water and sparkling white sands.

  2. Port Louis is the business and administrative capital of Mauritius. Get a sense of multi faith Mauritius here by gazing over churches, mosques, Chinese and Indian temples. Things to do in Port Louis:-

    • Visit Champ de Mars - the oldest racecourse in the Indian Ocean

    • Take a stroll down the Central Market to discover the market’s food court which offers the best Mauritian food experience

    • Spent a day at Le Caudan Waterfront which is a shopping and entertainment complex with restaurants, shops, a casino, a cinema, a craft market and a marina.


  3. Le Morne Brabant mountain is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is symbolic of the history of Mauritius. Located in the South West of the island, it is a place where slaves would hide while trying to escape. As you hike your way to the top of the mountain, you will discover numerous caves and be rewarded with a breath-taking view over the Indian Ocean.


  4. Casela Nature and Leisure Park is located in the Western part of Mauritius and is spread over an area of 14 hectares. The park is home to over 150 species of animals and reptiles such as lions, giraffes, birds and giant turtle. It also includes adventurous activities such as Segway and Quadbike tours, zip lining and hikings.


  5. Pamplemousses Botanical Garden also known as Sir Seewoosagur Ramgoolam Botanical Garden is named after the first Prime Minister of Mauritius. It is one of the oldest botanical gardens in the Southern hemisphere. The garden is most popular for its large water lilies, sugar canes and ebonies.


  6. Bois Cheri Tea Plantation is the largest tea producer in Mauritius and is also the site of a tea factory and tea museum. Guided tours of the tea plantation, factory and museum are available everyday.

There is no doubt that Mauritius is an extraordinary holiday destination; with outstanding weather practically all year round, an sensational culinary scene, superb attractions and a colourful and eclectic culture with wonderful locals.
